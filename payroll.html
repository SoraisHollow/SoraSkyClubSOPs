SoraSkyClub Income Distribution & Policy Guide

This page explains how SoraSkyClub distributes NET income from memberships, merchandise, and social media, and defines key terms and ownership policies.

1. Membership NET Income Distribution

Membership income is distributed from a shared Membership NET Income Pool. The pie chart in the original document shows how each staff group and SoraSkyClub receive a percentage of this pool. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

1.1 Percentage Breakdown (Membership NET Income)

Group Share of NET Membership Income Notes
Performers + Bartenders 15% Shared pool for all performers and bartenders.
Bouncers 20% Security / moderation staff.
Hosts 30% Event hosts and lead event staff.
World Devs 30% World creators / developers.
SoraSkyClub 5% SSC remainder for operations and reinvestment.

1.2 Example Membership Distribution

If SoraSkyClub earns $10,000 NET membership income, distribution is: :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

  • Performers + Bartenders (15%): $1,500
  • Bouncers (20%): $2,000
  • Hosts (30%): $3,000
  • World Devs (30%): $3,000
  • SoraSkyClub (5%): $500

1.3 Membership Pool Summary

  • All staff shares come from NET income (after fees & required expenses).
  • Performers + Bartenders share a combined 15% pool.
  • Bouncers receive 20%, Hosts 30%, World Devs 30%.
  • SoraSkyClub receives the remaining 5% of the membership pool.

2. Merchandise NET Profit Distribution

Merchandise based on artwork or brands uses a separate NET profit split. The pie chart in the PDF shows how Artist, Performer/Brand, and SoraSkyClub divide the revenue. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

2.1 Percentage Breakdown (Merch NET Profit)

Recipient Share of NET Merchandise Profit Notes
Artist 60% The creator of the artwork used on the product.
Performer / Brand 20% Performer or brand whose name/image is featured, if different from the artist.
SoraSkyClub 20% SSC remainder from merch NET profit.

2.2 Example Merchandise Distribution

If a product generates $2,000 NET profit, the split is: :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

  • Artist (60%): $1,200
  • Performer/Brand (20%): $400
  • SoraSkyClub (20%): $400

2.3 Special Case: Artist = Performer/Brand

When the Artist and Performer/Brand are the same person, they receive a unified total of 75% of the NET profit, with SoraSkyClub receiving the remainder. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

  • For $2,000 NET profit:
    • Artist = Performer/Brand (75%): $1,500
    • SoraSkyClub (25%): $500

2.4 Merchandise Revenue Policy Summary

  • Artists receive 60% NET profit on their artwork-based merchandise.
  • Performers/brands receive an additional 20%.
  • If performer = artist, they receive 75% total on that merch’s NET profit.
  • SoraSkyClub keeps the remainder for operations and reinvestment.

3. Social Media NET Income Distribution

Social media pages (such as dedicated SSC profiles or project accounts) have their own income split between the Social Media Manager and SoraSkyClub. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

3.1 Percentage Breakdown (Social Media NET Income)

Recipient Share of NET Income Notes
Social Media Manager 65% Manager assigned to that specific page/profile.
SoraSkyClub 35% SSC remainder from that page’s NET income.

3.2 Example Social Media Distribution

If a social page generates $1,000 NET, the split is: :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

  • Social Media Manager (65%): $650
  • SoraSkyClub (35%): $350

3.3 Social Media Manager Policy Summary

  • Managers receive 65% of the NET income generated by the social media pages they run.
  • NET income is always calculated after fees and required expenses.
  • Pages and assignments should be clearly documented so everyone knows who manages which profile.

4. IP Ownership & Key Definitions

4.1 IP Ownership Clause

All artwork and brand identity belong to the creator unless ownership is explicitly reassigned through a Terms of Service or contract. SoraSkyClub respects creator rights and only uses assets under the agreed terms. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

4.2 Definition – NET Income

NET Income means income after fees, processing costs, and required expenses are deducted. Examples of what comes out before NET is calculated:

  • Platform fees (e.g., store or payment processor fees).
  • Payment processing charges and taxes as applicable.
  • Production, shipping, or fulfillment costs for merchandise.
  • Any other required expenses tied directly to generating that income.

All percentages and examples in this policy refer to NET income, not gross revenue. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

This income policy page is a living document. If SoraSkyClub’s structure or agreements change, the percentages and examples here may be updated to match new arrangements. When in doubt, leadership should clarify how a specific project, collab, or product fits within these rules.
