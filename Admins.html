Admin Review SOP (Events & Infractions)

This page explains how Admins should review event entries and moderation cases in Notion, decide when follow-up is needed, and make sure Hosts and Bouncers are following their procedures.

Role: Admins are quality control. You do not replace Hosts or Bouncers; you verify that they have done their job correctly, triage by severity, close cases, and handle escalations when needed.

0. Overview of Responsibilities

  • Regularly review Events in Notion for completeness and consistency.
  • Regularly review Moderation / Cases for accuracy and enough evidence.
  • Triage by severity: mark cases as Critical (needs immediate attention) or Non-severe (can be handled during normal admin review).
  • Decide whether each event or case Needs follow-up, Can be closed, or Must be escalated.
  • Provide feedback to Hosts and Bouncers if procedures are not followed correctly.
  • Update admin-only fields and statuses in Notion to keep records clean.

  • Description: Clear event name/theme.

2. Moderation Case Review (Bouncer Work)

Goal: Ensure that every moderation action is properly logged, fair, backed by evidence, and that cases are handled in order of severity.

2.1 What to Review

In the Moderation / Cases database, filter for:

  • Cases with no Admin status or marked Pending/Needs Review.
  • Recent cases (e.g. last 30 days), with Critical ones checked first.

2.2 Case Quality Checklist

  • Case Title: Quick, clear synopsis of what happened.
  • Case ID: Uses the correct format: Case-{Year}-{sequential number}.
  • Users: All involved users are listed, with correct names.
  • Action Taken: All actions are recorded (warnings, mutes, kicks, bans, escalations).
  • Short Reason: Appropriate violation tags selected.
  • Date: Incident date is filled in and reasonable.
  • Evidence:
    • Screenshots attached directly to the Evidence field where possible.
    • Video links pointing to #üõÖ„Éªevidence-locker with the correct Case ID.
  • Internal Notes: Provide enough context to understand the situation.
  • Severity (Admin-only field): Set to Critical or Non-severe based on impact and urgency.

2.3 Severity & Deciding Follow-Up

Critical Cases (Immediate Attention)

  • Threats, doxxing, extreme harassment, hate content, or safety concerns.
  • Repeat severe offenses or long histories of issues with the same user.
  • Incidents happening during or right before major events.

Mark Severity = Critical. Review these cases first, ping relevant staff/Owner if needed, and decide quickly whether to uphold, increase, or change the action taken.

Non-Severe Cases (Standard Review)

  • Minor rule breaks already handled with warnings or short mutes.
  • First-time, low-impact offenses.
  • Cases where the user corrected behavior after staff guidance.

Mark Severity = Non-severe. These can be processed during normal admin review sessions without urgent escalation.

2.4 Close, Follow-Up, or Escalate

Cases That Can Be Closed

  • Evidence supports the action taken.
  • Violation severity matches the response.
  • Procedures were followed correctly by the Bouncer.

Mark the case with an admin-only field such as Status = Closed and optionally note ‚ÄúReviewed by Admin ‚Äì Date‚Äù.

Cases That Need Follow-Up

  • Evidence is incomplete or missing.
  • Action taken seems too harsh or too light for the violation.
  • There is a pattern of repeated behavior by the same user.
  • A Bouncer skipped steps or logged the case incorrectly.

Set Status = Needs Follow-Up and add a clear Admin note: what needs to happen next, who owns the follow-up, and whether the case is Critical or Non-severe.

2.5 Escalation

  • For severe incidents (hate speech, doxxing, threats, serious harassment):
    • Mark Severity = Critical and Status = Escalated.
    • Ping the Owner / HR-style contact in the staff channels.
    • Ensure all evidence is safely stored and linked.
  • Document final decisions in an admin-only notes field (ban length, appeal outcome, etc.).

3. Checking Staff Procedure Quality

Goal: Make sure Hosts and Bouncers are consistently using their SOPs.

3.1 Hosts (Events)

  • Are events added to Notion in a timely manner?
  • Are VRChat, Discord, and Fourthwall pieces in place before ‚ÄúPosted?‚Äù is checked?
  • Do descriptions and times match across all platforms?
  • Is there clear communication in #üóíÔ∏è„Éªplanning when something changes?

3.2 Bouncers (Moderation Logging)

  • Are all significant incidents getting case files?
  • Are Case IDs formatted correctly and sequential?
  • Is evidence properly attached and easy to read?
  • Is Severity being set appropriately (Critical vs Non-severe)?
  • Are Internal Notes professional and neutral in tone?

3.3 Giving Feedback

  • For minor issues, DM the staff member with a friendly correction (e.g. ‚ÄúNext time, please‚Ä¶‚Äù).
  • For repeated issues, document them in admin notes and bring them to staff meetings.
  • Update SOP pages when patterns show the process needs clarification.

4. Quick Admin Checklist

  • Review upcoming Events in Notion at regular intervals.
  • Verify each event has correct details, links, and a live Fourthwall post.
  • Mark events clearly when they are fully posted/approved.
  • Review new Moderation Cases, starting with those marked Critical.
  • Ensure each case has a Severity set (Critical or Non-severe).
  • Check that evidence (screenshots & video links) is properly connected to each case.
  • Decide: Closed, Needs Follow-Up, or Escalated for every pending case.
  • Give feedback to Hosts and Bouncers when SOPs are not being followed.
  • Keep admin-only fields and notes up to date so the whole leadership team has a clear picture.

Admin work is what keeps SoraSkyClub organized, safe, and consistent. If something in a process is unclear or repeatedly causing issues, document it and bring it up so the SOPs can evolve with the community.
